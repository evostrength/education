The Earthquake Bar

Compromising stability to improve your strength and technique.

Written by Daniel Frazier on July 19, 2018

The material composition and uniform force distribution of a traditional barbell and plate system allow for a predictiable trajectory given a force input on the system by a trainee. Let's use the bench press as an example. A trainee can quite accurately predict the location that a barbell will end up in after he/she applies a force to the barbell during the concentric (pressing) portion of the bench press. This is due to a combination of years of experience honing natural human coordination and the rigid composition of the barbell and plate system.

On the contrary, the material composition of the earthquake bar is designed to increase the unpredictability of motion given a force input. This is due in part to the flexible characteristic of the bar's carbon fiber construction. This unpredictibility is further amplified by the use of weights that are hanging from rubber bands in a pendulum-like fashion. The rubber bands allow unpredictable motion of the weights in all three dimenions, and the potential energy that is stored in the bands is ever changing throughout the bench stroke.

The Earthquake Bar

This unpredictability leads to joint instability and promotes the use and engagement of muscles responsible for the stabilization of the elbow and shoulder joints. These muscles play a larger roll in movement and stability when the force exerted on them is unpredictable.

Benefits

Helps improve joint stability -> Reduce chance of injury

Helps improves form in traditional pressing movements

Conclusion

If you are interested in training with the earthquake bar, feel free to ask a staff member for a demonstration of proper use and technique.
